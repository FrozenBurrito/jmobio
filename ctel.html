  • Language & Language Development March, 2021

    This data-science project was approved by Professor Oler as an alternative final project in Language & Language Development. The source code, datasets, results, and explanation are available on Kaggle, a data science community.

    I used the 2018 and 2019 CA Summative ELPAC research datasets (for all CA) to train machine learning models to help answer questions such as: Does speaking performance have predictive value for listening performance? If we know the % of students at a given school that fall within the begin, moderate, and developed band for Speaking, can a machine learning model predict the % of students scoring within the corresponding bands for Listening?

    My goal was to explore connections between the learning processes/skills tested by the ELPAC (Speaking, Listening, Reading, Writing). Fourteen (14) machine learning models were trained and tested -- 1 for each pair (Speaking predicting Listening, Listening Predicting Speaking, etc.) plus 2 others for Overall Writ Lang predicting Overall Oral Lang (a close connection) and vice versa.

    The most interesting results indicate a connection between Reading and Listening that appears to be closer than other language process pairs. I speculate that this could be related to subvocablization.



    I am interested in revising and expanding this project in the future.
